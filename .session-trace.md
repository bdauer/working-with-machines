### Voice-calibrated review (4 parallel reviewers)
First run of the skill concept (manually, before registration). Four voice-focused perspectives: voice match, agentic tics, structural voice, register/inflation. All calibrated against voice samples.

Key learnings for the skill:
- **Voice match reviewer was strongest.** Comparing against actual voice samples produced the most specific, actionable findings. Found that whats-changing.md has the most voice drift — the raw voice sample for the same ideas is rougher and more committed.
- **Structural voice reviewer caught unique patterns** (abstract-before-concrete, missing closers, too-smooth flow, over-structured bullets) that other reviewers missed.
- **Register/inflation reviewer** was precise on individual words ("cross-pollinate", "distinctive", "Focus degradation") and spotted repeated phrases across files ("enters deeply into" x4, "earning its length" x3).
- **Tics reviewer** was thorough on oxford commas (9 instances) and mechanical rhythm (workflow opening formula, agent-patterns template rigidity).

### Skill refinement
Learnings integrated into skill body (not appended — integrated into existing sections per editorial guide's own "integrate, don't append" rule): voice match always included, structure catches unique patterns, oxford comma nuance in reviewer instructions, uncertain-vs-definite framing for sample gaps.
